IronGauge — Privacy Policy

Last updated: 12 July 2026

IronGauge is an offline-first training and nutrition tracker. It is designed so that you are the only custodian of your data. The developer operates no servers, and cannot see, collect, or access anything you record.

Data stored on your device

Everything you log — workouts, foods, body weight, training plans, and settings — is stored locally on your device in your browser's storage. It never leaves your device unless you export it yourself or enable the optional Google Drive backup described below.

Optional Google Drive backup

You can connect your own Google account so IronGauge keeps automatic backups of your data. If you do:

  • IronGauge requests a single permission scope: drive.appdata. This gives access only to a hidden application-data folder that IronGauge itself creates in your Google Drive. It cannot see, read, or modify any of your other Drive files.
  • The only data written there are backup files containing the same information you see in the app (your logs, plans, and settings), so that you can restore them later.
  • Google sign-in tokens are held in memory only and are not stored by the app.
  • Backups are stored in your Google account, under Google's own security. The developer has no access to them.

IronGauge's use of information received from Google APIs adheres to the Google API Services User Data Policy, including the Limited Use requirements. Data obtained through Google APIs is used only to provide the user-facing backup and restore feature, and is never transferred to anyone else, used for advertising, or used to train models.

Deleting your data

  • On this device: uninstall the app or clear the site's browsing data.
  • Cloud backups: use Stats → Cloud backup → Delete cloud backups inside the app, or revoke IronGauge's access at myaccount.google.com/permissions and remove hidden app data via Google Drive's settings (Settings → Manage apps).
  • Disconnecting (Stats → Cloud backup → Disconnect) stops all backup activity immediately; existing backups remain in your Drive until you delete them.

What the developer collects

Nothing. No accounts, no analytics, no tracking, no advertising, no crash reporting, no data sale, no data sharing. The app is a static web page served from GitHub Pages; GitHub's own privacy statement covers ordinary web-server logs involved in delivering the page.

Children

IronGauge is a general-audience fitness tool and is not directed at children under 13.
